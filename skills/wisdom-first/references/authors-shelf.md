# The author's shelf — a personal taste layer (NOT part of the skill's reasoning)

This file is OPTIONAL and SEPARATE from the skill's judgment logic in
`the-taste.md`. The taste file decides, by pure general principles, the best two or
three works for any need — it names no specific book and knows no domains. This
shelf lets the skill's *author* hand-seed a few personal favorites, so that anyone
who installs the skill also benefits from the author's tested taste.

It exists for two honest reasons:

1. A few excellent works are quieter than their famous neighbors — they sit low in
   the common prior, so even perfect general taste won't reliably surface them.
   (Tested: no purely-general phrasing reliably reaches such a book; trying only
   distorts the picks for unrelated problems.)
2. Sharing a skill is also a way to share a point of view about what's good. A
   shelf lets an author pass their taste along, on purpose.

## The one rule the skill applies (general — not a lookup table)

During step 1, **after** `the-taste.md` has produced a complementary candidate set
by pure judgment: if a shelf entry is a strong fit for the **same need-dimension**
as one of those candidate slots, prefer the shelf work in that slot and mark it
"(author's pick)".

This is a **tie-break** among works the taste has already judged equally fitting —
never an override. If a shelf entry does not genuinely fit the live need, its scope,
and its dimension, ignore it. The shelf never adds a domain, never forces a book
onto a problem it doesn't fit, and never changes the judgments. The general engine
still chooses the set; the shelf only nudges one slot toward a favorite the author
trusts. Everything not on the shelf is chosen by `the-taste.md` alone.
